Weiyu Liu

I am a Robotics Ph.D. student at Georgia Institute of Technology, where I am advised by professor Sonia Chernova. In the summer of 2021, I interned at Nvidia Research, working with Dieter Fox, Tucker Hermans, and Chris Paxton. Prior to joining the PhD program, I received my Bachelorâ€™s degree in Electrical Engineering from Georgia Tech.

My goal is to develop robots that can effectively perceive, model, and interact with objects in the real world. Towards this goal, I study the representation and reasoning of semantic knowledge, which can concisely describe target domains with symbols and reveal meaningful patterns shared between problems and environments (e.g., clean cups are likely to be found in kitchen cabinets) . My research develops semantic reasoning frameworks capable of modeling complex semantic knowledge grounded in robot perception and action.
